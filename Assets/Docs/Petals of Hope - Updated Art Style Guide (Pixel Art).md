# Petals of Hope - Art Style Guide (Pixel Art)

## Visual Style
*Petals of Hope* embraces a vibrant pixel art style with a retro-inspired, 16-bit aesthetic. Sprites and environments are designed with a base resolution of 16x16 or 32x32 pixels, balancing nostalgic charm with modern clarity. This approach ensures expressive animations and detailed settings suitable for a 2D platformer.

## Color Palette
Each environment features a limited, evocative color palette to maintain cohesion and enhance the retro feel:
- **Forest**: Lush greens, deep browns, and bright flower accents (reds, yellows). Shadows use cooler greens for depth.
- **Desert**: Warm oranges, sandy yellows, and burnt reds, paired with deep blue skies and occasional green oasis highlights.
- **Mountain**: Cool blues, grays, and whites for snow and ice, with darker shadows and warm sunset tones.
- **Cave**: Dark purples, blues, and blacks, contrasted by glowing neon colors (pinks, greens) for crystals and bioluminescent plants.

## Character Design
- **Elias**: A small, determined sprite in a simple tunic and boots. His everyman charm is highlighted by a distinctive hat or scarf. Animations include walking, jumping, and dashing.
- **Enemies**:
  - **Forest**: Angular wolves, spindly-legged spiders, and archer elves with bows and quivers.
  - **Desert**: Segmented-tailed scorpions, emerging sandworms, and cloaked bandits with daggers.
  - **Mountain**: Bulky yetis with fur-like textures, eagles with spread wings, and blocky rock golems.
  - **Cave**: Flapping bats, splitting slimes, and shadow creatures with glowing eyes.
- **Bosses**: Larger sprites (e.g., 64x64 pixels) with intricate animations and unique attack patterns.

## Environment Design
- **Backgrounds**: Built with tiled pixel art and parallax scrolling for depth. Layers use desaturated colors to suggest distance.
  - **Forest**: Dense trees, vines, and faint foliage.
  - **Desert**: Rolling dunes, distant ruins, and heat haze effects.
  - **Mountain**: Jagged cliffs, snowy peaks, and cloudy skies.
  - **Cave**: Stalactites, glowing crystals, and shadowy recesses.
- **Interactive Elements**: Platforms, traps, and hazards blend into the environment (e.g., mossy platforms in the forest, sandstone blocks in the desert).
- **Lighting**: Achieved with color gradients and dithering, brighter in the forest and desert, moodier in the mountain and cave.

## UI Elements
- **HUD**: Pixelated icons for health (hearts), coin counters, and abilities (e.g., a double arrow for dash).
- **Menus**: Retro-style menus with blocky fonts and bordered windows. The main menu showcases a pixel art title screen featuring Elias and the magical flower.
- **Pause Menu**: A semi-transparent pixel overlay with options like Resume, Restart Level, and Quit, matching the game’s style.
